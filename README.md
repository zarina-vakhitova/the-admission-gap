# The Admission Gap

An interactive dashboard for the paper:

> Vakhitova, Z., Mezzetti, C., Alston-Knox, C., Jowett, S., Rhind, D., Lin, C.-Y., Huang, P.-C., Vergani, M., & O'Brien, K. (2026). *Performance-enhancing drug use in sports: a Bayesian variable selection and profile regression approach to identifying risk factors and estimating prevalence.* Substance Abuse Treatment, Prevention, and Policy, 21:25.
> [doi.org/10.1186/s13011-025-00699-x](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13011-025-00699-x)

The paper introduces a Bayesian two-step method (BVS + BPR) for estimating the prevalence of behaviours people won't admit on surveys. Validated on 2,113 UK university athletes, it shifts the prevalence estimate of performance-enhancing drug use from 3.4% (self-report) to 13.7% — a 4× undercount.
